Abstract

We propose a novel neural codec-based framework for mono-to-binaural speech synthesis that disentangles and separately models spatial, acoustic, and semantic information. Our approach employs a multi-branch architecture with three parallel encoding-decoding pathways: (1) a Spatial Branch that captures interaural time and level differences (ITD/ILD) from the binaural spatial cues, (2) an Acoustic Branch that processes the monaural speech signal and generates left/right ear outputs through sum-and-difference decoding, and (3) a Semantic Branch that leverages frozen HuBERT features to preserve speech content. Each branch utilizes Residual Vector Quantization (RVQ-4) operating at 25Hz to obtain discrete representations, which are then concatenated and jointly decoded. The model is trained with spatial, acoustic, and semantic reconstruction losses to ensure high-fidelity binaural audio synthesis. Experimental results demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance on both in-domain and out-of-domain datasets.

Model Overview

Figure 1: Overview of the proposed framework. The model consists of three branches: Spatial Branch (top, blue) for encoding spatial cues, Acoustic Branch (middle, cyan) for processing monaural audio and generating binaural outputs, and Semantic Branch (bottom, yellow) for preserving speech content using HuBERT features. All branches employ RVQ-4 for discrete representation learning.
